Patrick Garvin: The web content accessibility guidelines are a collection of guidelines based on four principles. Those principles are what are called the POUR principles: Perceivable, operable, understandable and robust. These principles are the basis of web content accessibility efforts across the world. The web content accessibility guidelines are often referred to as WCAG, or W-C-A-G. They have evolved over the last 23 years, and they are going to continue to evolve and grow.
